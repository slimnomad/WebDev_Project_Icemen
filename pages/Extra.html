Almaty

Explore the dramatic Tien Shan range with guided excursions that blend cultural experiences and high-mountain trekking. This trip focuses on acclimatisation and immersive local hospitality.

  • 3-night stay in a mountain guesthouse with full-board
  • Guided hiking, acclimatisation walks, and route briefing
  • Transport from Almaty and local transfers
  • Optional equipment rental and porter assistance

Recommended for: Fit trekkers and cultural explorers who enjoy remote mountain villages.

Safety & fitness: Moderate to high fitness required; we maintain small group sizes and provide local guides trained in mountain rescue.

Why book: A unique blend of high-altitude landscapes, welcoming local culture, and expert support for first-time trekkers to the Tien Shan.

Spotlight: Blind Winter Skateboarders

Blind and visually impaired athletes are redefining what is possible in winter sports. Their courage, skill, and determination inspire communities and prove that barriers can be overcome with the right support and mindset. From mastering challenging slopes to competing at elite levels, these athletes show that vision is not a limitation to greatness. Their stories highlight the power of inclusion, adaptive techniques, and the human spirit.

Here are a few examples of remarkable blind winter athletes:

Brian Mckeever

Brian McKeever is a Canadian Paralympic cross-country skier who has competed at six Winter Games. Diagnosed with Stargardt disease at age 19, he lost central vision but went on to become one of the most decorated Winter Paralympians, winning 20 medals, including 16 golds.
"obstacles only become barriers if you let them" - Brian Kneever

Menna Fitzpatrick

Menna Fitzpatrick is a British Paralympic alpine skier with only about 5% vision due to congenital retinal folds. She is Britain’s most decorated Winter Paralympian, winning multiple medals at the 2018 and 2022 Games, and has inspired many with her determination, skill, and resilience.
"If I inspire just one person then I feel my job is done"

Oksana Shyshkova

Oksana Shyshkova is a Ukrainian Paralympic biathlete and cross-country skier competing in the visually impaired class (B2). She has won multiple Paralympic medals, including gold at the 2018 and 2022 Games, and has been a leading figure in world Para Nordic skiing.
"It's totally crazy. I can only explain it as crazy because I did not expect to win.""
